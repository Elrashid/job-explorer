# Literature Search Criteria

## Purpose
Papers needed for the literature review section of our article:
**"AI-Driven Analysis of Job Market Demands in the MENA Region: A Systematic Review of LinkedIn Job Postings Using Large Language Models"**

Please search Google Scholar, arXiv, IEEE Xplore, ACM Digital Library, and Scopus for papers matching these criteria. For each paper found, provide: title, authors, year, DOI (or arXiv ID), and a 1-line summary.

---

## Search Category 1: Job Market Analysis Using Web Scraping / NLP

**Search queries:**
- `"job posting analysis" AND ("natural language processing" OR "NLP" OR "text mining")`
- `"labor market" AND "web scraping" AND ("skill demand" OR "skill gap")`
- `"LinkedIn" AND "job posting" AND "analysis"`
- `"online job advertisement" AND "text analysis"`

**What we need:** Studies that analyzed job postings (from LinkedIn, Indeed, etc.) using automated methods to extract skills, requirements, or market trends. Especially interested in methodology papers that describe their extraction pipeline.

**Key papers to find (if they exist):**
- Any study specifically analyzing LinkedIn job data
- Burning Glass / EMSI labor market analytics methodology papers
- O*NET skills taxonomy studies

---

## Search Category 2: MENA Region Labor Market / Skills Gap

**Search queries:**
- `"MENA" AND ("labor market" OR "job market") AND ("skills" OR "digital transformation")`
- `"Gulf countries" AND ("employment" OR "workforce") AND ("AI" OR "technology")`
- `"UAE" AND ("job market" OR "employment trends") AND "technology"`
- `"GCC" AND "digital skills" AND ("gap" OR "demand")`
- `"Saudi Arabia" OR "Qatar" OR "UAE" AND "Vision 2030" AND "workforce"`

**What we need:** Studies on employment trends, skills gaps, or digital transformation in the MENA/GCC region. Reports from McKinsey, World Economic Forum, or government bodies about Gulf workforce transformation.

---

## Search Category 3: LLMs for Information Extraction

**Search queries:**
- `"large language model" AND "information extraction" AND ("structured data" OR "named entity")`
- `"GPT" OR "Claude" OR "LLM" AND "text classification" AND "job" OR "resume"`
- `"LLM" AND "data extraction" AND "unstructured text"`
- `"prompt engineering" AND "structured output" AND "extraction"`

**What we need:** Papers demonstrating LLMs (GPT-4, Claude, LLaMA, etc.) used for extracting structured information from unstructured text. Especially: reliability studies, comparison with traditional NLP, prompt engineering for extraction tasks.

---

## Search Category 4: PRISMA in Non-Medical Systematic Reviews

**Search queries:**
- `"PRISMA" AND ("systematic review" OR "SLR") AND ("computer science" OR "information systems" OR "software engineering")`
- `"PRISMA" AND "non-medical" AND "systematic review"`
- `"systematic literature review" AND "job posting" OR "labor market"`

**What we need:** Examples of PRISMA methodology applied outside medicine — in CS, IS, or social sciences. This validates our methodological choice.

**Key reference:**
- Page, M. J., et al. (2021). The PRISMA 2020 statement: an updated guideline for reporting systematic reviews. *BMJ*, 372, n71. DOI: 10.1136/bmj.n71

---

## Search Category 5: Career Path Modeling / Skills Taxonomy

**Search queries:**
- `"career path" AND "modeling" AND ("machine learning" OR "data-driven")`
- `"skills taxonomy" AND ("job posting" OR "labor market")`
- `"career transition" AND "skill gap" AND "analysis"`
- `"occupational mobility" AND "skills" AND "data"`

**What we need:** Papers on data-driven career path analysis, skills-based job matching, or occupational mobility research. Studies that cluster jobs by skill requirements.

---

## Search Category 6: AI/Tech Workforce Demand Studies

**Search queries:**
- `"artificial intelligence" AND "workforce demand" AND "skills"`
- `"AI skills gap" AND ("2024" OR "2025" OR "2026")`
- `"technology job market" AND "trends" AND "analysis"`

**What we need:** Recent reports/papers on AI workforce demand globally. World Economic Forum Future of Jobs reports, LinkedIn Workforce reports, etc.

## Priority Papers (Known References to Find Full Citations)

These are foundational — please find their full APA citations with DOIs:

1. **PRISMA 2020 Statement** — Page et al. (2021) - BMJ
2. **World Economic Forum Future of Jobs Report** (2023 or 2025 edition)
3. **LinkedIn Economic Graph** — any methodology papers from LinkedIn's research team

## Output Format

For each paper found, please provide in this format:

```
### [Short Label]
- **Title:** ...
- **Authors:** ...
- **Year:** ...
- **Source:** Journal/Conference name
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/...  (or arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/...)
- **Summary:** 1-2 sentence description of relevance
```

Aim for **15-25 papers total** across all categories. Quality over quantity — we need papers that directly support our methodology and findings.
